About Gaya Ji – The Land of Liberation

Gaya Ji holds immense significance in Hinduism as the place where Lord Rama, along with Sita and Lakshmana, performed Pind Daan for King Dasharatha. It is believed that performing Shraddha rituals here brings salvation to ancestors.

Pitru Paksha Mela is a 16-day period dedicated to honoring ancestors through Pind Daan rituals. During this sacred time, thousands of devotees gather in Gaya to perform these rites for their departed loved ones.

"In Gaya, every offering becomes a bridge to eternity."

History & Beliefs of Pind Daan

The origin of Pind Daan traces back to Garuda Purana and other ancient scriptures. According to legend, Lord Vishnu's footprint (Vishnupad) in Gaya makes it the most sacred place for performing ancestral rites.

It is believed that when Pind Daan is performed in Gaya, the offerings directly reach the ancestors, freeing them from any unresolved earthly attachments and granting them moksha (liberation).

The ancient beliefs behind Pitru Paksha emphasize that during these 16 days, the veil between worlds is thinnest, allowing our prayers and offerings to reach our ancestors directly.
